        Welcome to the Employee Information System, where efficiency meets empowerment! This project provides a comprehensive solution for businesses to manage their workforce with ease and flexibility.


Overview

        Imagine this: You're a busy boss overseeing a thriving company with a diverse team of talented individuals. With this Employee Information System, you have the power to effortlessly navigate through your employee database, making strategic decisions that drive growth and success. Upon landing on the homepage, you're greeted with a sleek interface that allows you to filter employees by job title and gender, giving you instant access to the information you need. Want to find all female software engineers? No problem. Looking for male engineers? Done.

        But I don't stop there. I understand that as the boss, you need to make informed decisions about promotions and salary increases. That's why I've implemented an innovative feature that allows you to handpick the employees you believe deserve recognition and reward. Simply select the individuals you want to promote, and their names, positions, and salaries will be displayed prominently, putting their achievements in the spotlight. In addition, I've included a real-time counter that shows you the total number of selected employees, ensuring that you always have a clear understanding of your decisions' impact. But what if you change your mind or want to start fresh? No problem. With just a click of a button, you can reset all search conditions, giving you the freedom to explore new possibilities without any hassle.

  2. Summary

            Considering design principles, the interface of Amazon shopping cart is simple, and the operation is simple, allowing users to operate it intuitively without learning. So, I think that he "most successful" would likely be Amazon shopping cart due to its seamless integration, robust functionality, and alignment with user expectations in an e-commerce environment. However, the "worst" among them is subjective and depends on specific user needs and preferences. For example, someone who values simplicity might prefer Google bookmarks, while a frequent YouTube user might prioritize the customization options of YouTube's video list despite its navigation challenges. Therefore, the determination of the "worst" design depends on individual user context and priorities.

  3. Features that I plan to implement

            I think that Google bookmarks follows the design principle of simplicity. Its interface is clean and simple, focusing on the core functionality of bookmarking web pages without unnecessary clutter. Furthermore, in YouTube, as the number of videos in the playlist increases, browsing them can become challenging. For my design, I hope that simplicity can be integrated into the interface I design, so that the page can display the content aggregated by users concisely and clearly. In addition, in my development, I hope that I can display the user's search content through the filtering function, thereby improving usage efficiency.


Functionality

  1. Two filtering categories: Users can filter based on gender and position.

  2. Sorting button: Users can sort employees' salaries from low to high.

  3. Reset button: Users can clear all sorting and filtering criteria by clicking this button.

  4. Add/Remove button: Users can add/remove employees to/from the list by clicking this button.

  5. A list used to count the selected employees, with a counter to count the total number of employees.
